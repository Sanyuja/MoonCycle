The Reset

"The dam is full. The red flag is out. It's carnage time." 😂

This phase is for rest, not punishment. Your body is already working hard enough. Honor that. OMAD (one meal a day) is done at most twice this week. The focus is gentle movement, warmth, and deep restoration.

New moon energy matches perfectly: dark, inward, stillness. This is where you release and begin again.

The Rise

"Energy returns, strength increases, motivation flows."

This is where you come alive. 4 workouts per week, with legs as the non-negotiable foundation every other day. Arms, core, and cardio fill the remaining days. Always yoga the morning after each workout. Always train at sunset. 🌅

The waxing moon builds to the full moon peak at ovulation. Peak moon, peak strength, peak power. This is your time.

The Wind Down

"I used to fight it. Now I honor it."

Motivation dips, the body feels heavier, the mind gets foggy. And that is perfect. No heavy lifting, no pushing through. This week is yoga and sauna only.

The waning moon mirrors this exactly: releasing, softening, turning inward. The sauna is medicine. The yoga is prayer. Together they carry you gently back to day zero.

🌙 The Philosophy

My body is not a machine that performs the same output every day. She is cyclical, seasonal, rhythmic. When I stopped trying to be consistent in the conventional sense, same workout, same intensity, every day, and started being consistent with my cycle instead, everything changed.

I am stronger. I recover faster. I actually enjoy moving my body. Working out according to my cycle is not a hack. It is just listening.

"Rejecting the collective's script and writing your own." 👑
